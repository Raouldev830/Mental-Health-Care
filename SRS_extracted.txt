1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the complete functional and non-functional requirements for the Integrated Mental Health and Infant Healthcare Management System (IMHIHMS) for the Republic of Cameroon. The document is intended to serve as the authoritative reference for design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance of the system.

This SRS is prepared in accordance with IEEE Std 830-1998 (IEEE Recommended Practice for Software Requirements Specifications) and aligned with the World Health Organization (WHO) mhGAP Intervention Guide, the Cameroon National Health Development Plan (PNDS 2021–2025), and UNICEF's child health frameworks.

1.2 Scope

The IMHIHMS is a digital health platform designed to improve healthcare delivery for two priority population groups in Cameroon:

Mental Health Patients: individuals experiencing psychiatric disorders, depression, anxiety, substance use disorders, epilepsy, and other neurological conditions.

Infants and Children (0–5 years): covering antenatal care tracking, neonatal screening, immunisation management, growth monitoring, and malnutrition detection.

The system will be deployed across public health facilities nationwide — district hospitals, health centres, and community health posts — with priority phasing in the ten regions. It will operate in connected, low-bandwidth, and offline environments.

Out of scope: private hospital billing systems, adult surgical care, dental care, and any clinical decision-making not covered by mhGAP or EPI protocols.

1.5 Overview of Document

The remainder of this document is structured as follows: Section 2 provides the overall product description including context and constraints; Section 3 describes stakeholders and user classes; Sections 4 and 5 define functional and non-functional requirements respectively; Sections 6–8 cover architecture, interfaces, and data requirements; Sections 9–10 address security, compliance, constraints, and assumptions; and Sections 11–12 provide the glossary and appendices.

2. Overall Description

2.1 Product Perspective

IMHIHMS is a new, purpose-built system that will integrate with Cameroon's existing Health Information System (HIS), specifically the District Health Information Software 2 (DHIS2) instance maintained by MINSANTE. It does not replace DHIS2 but supplements it with specialised clinical workflow modules not currently available in the national platform.

The system operates as a web-based application accessible from desktop browsers and as a Progressive Web App (PWA) on Android and iOS mobile devices. A lightweight Android APK will also be provided for facilities with very limited connectivity.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)

At a high level, the system provides the following major capabilities:

Patient registration and unique national health identifier (NHI) management.

Mental health screening, diagnosis support (mhGAP-aligned), treatment planning, and follow-up.

Infant health record management including antenatal care, birth registration, EPI immunisation schedule, growth monitoring, and IMCI assessment.

Appointment scheduling and case follow-up for both modules.

Community health worker (CHW) field data collection with offline sync.

Aggregate and individual reporting, dashboards, and analytics.

Referral management between community, district, and regional levels.

Role-based access control and comprehensive audit logging.

Integration with DHIS2 for aggregate national reporting.

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics

See Section 3 for a detailed stakeholder and user class analysis. Primary user classes include: Clinical Staff (psychiatrists, paediatricians, nurses), Community Health Workers, Health Facility Administrators, Regional Health Officers, and National Administrators.

2.4 Operating Environment

Web browsers: Chrome 110+, Firefox 110+, Edge 110+ (desktop and Android).

Mobile OS: Android 8.0+ (primary), iOS 14+ (secondary).

Server OS: Ubuntu 22.04 LTS on-premise or cloud-hosted (AWS/Azure/local data centre).

Database: PostgreSQL 14+.

Network: Must function over GPRS (2G, ~50 kbps) in offline-first mode; full functionality requires 3G or better.

Electricity: Must tolerate intermittent power; client devices must cache data locally for at least 72 hours of offline operation.

2.5 Design and Implementation Constraints

All patient data must be stored on servers physically located within the Republic of Cameroon.

The user interface must support both French and English (Cameroon's two official languages) as primary languages; support for Fulfulde and Pidgin English is desirable.

The system must be compatible with the national DHIS2 instance using the DHIS2 Web API v2.39+.

The system must comply with the WHO mhGAP Intervention Guide v2.0 clinical protocols.

Open-source or government-licensed software components must be preferred to minimise licensing costs.

The initial deployment budget constrains infrastructure to a maximum of two dedicated application servers and one database server at the national level.

2.6 Assumptions and Dependencies

MINSANTE will provide a unique National Health Identifier (NHI) scheme; if unavailable at launch, the system will generate a temporary local identifier with a migration path.

DHIS2 national instance remains available for aggregate data export.

Participating health facilities have at least one Android device per module.

Training will be provided to all end users prior to go-live.

Uninterrupted power supply (UPS) or solar backup is available at district hospitals.

3.2 User Classes

3.2.1 Clinical Staff (Psychiatrists, Paediatricians, Nurses)

Highest-privilege clinical users. Work in health facilities with moderate to good connectivity. Require fast access to patient records, clinical decision support, and the ability to enter diagnoses, prescriptions, and referrals. Estimated: ~2,000 users nationally at full rollout.

3.2.2 Community Health Workers (CHWs)

Field-based, semi-literate to literate users operating on mobile devices in remote areas with limited or no connectivity. Require simplified, icon-driven forms in local languages, full offline capability, and guided workflows. Estimated: ~15,000 users nationally.

3.2.3 Health Facility Administrators

Responsible for facility-level configuration, user account management, appointment scheduling oversight, and local reporting. Medium technical literacy. Estimated: ~500 users.

3.2.4 Regional / District Health Officers

Supervisory users who review aggregate data, monitor KPIs, and produce reports for regional delegations. Access dashboards and export tools. Estimated: ~200 users.

3.2.5 National Administrators (MINSANTE IT)

Super-administrators with access to system configuration, national analytics, DHIS2 integration management, and audit logs. Estimated: ~10 users.

3.2.6 Patients / Caregivers (Future Phase)

A patient-facing portal / mobile app is planned for Phase 2, allowing caregivers to view immunisation schedules, appointment reminders, and educational content. Requirements for this class are deferred to SRS v2.0.

6. System Architecture Overview

6.1 High-Level Architecture

The IMHIHMS adopts a three-tier web application architecture with the following layers:

Presentation Tier: React-based Single Page Application (SPA) / Progressive Web App (PWA), plus an Android APK for offline-first CHW usage.

Application Tier: RESTful API server (Node.js or Django REST Framework), hosting business logic, clinical decision support rules, and integration services.

Data Tier: PostgreSQL 14+ relational database for structured clinical data; object storage (MinIO or equivalent) for documents and images.

A Redis cache layer will be deployed between the application and data tiers to manage session state and frequently accessed reference data (EPI schedules, drug formularies, ICD-11 codes).

6.2 Deployment Architecture

Phase 1 deployment will consist of: one primary application server, one database server, and one hot-standby database replica — all hosted in a Cameroonian data centre. A reverse proxy / load balancer (Nginx) will sit in front of the application server. A dedicated SMS gateway service will handle outbound appointment and vaccination reminders.

Phase 2 will introduce a second application server behind the load balancer and transition to a containerised Kubernetes deployment for improved resilience.

6.3 Integration Architecture

DHIS2 Integration: Aggregate indicator exports via the DHIS2 Web API (v2.39+) using a scheduled batch job. No real-time integration is required in Phase 1.

SMS Gateway: Integration with a local Cameroonian SMS aggregator (e.g., Orange Cameroun API or a country-compliant aggregator) for appointment and vaccination reminders.

National NHI Registry: REST API integration when the national registry becomes available; stub implementation for Phase 1.

7. External Interface Requirements

7.1 User Interfaces

The web application shall use a responsive layout supporting screen widths from 360px (small Android phones) to 1920px (desktop monitors).

The CHW mobile interface shall be a separate, simplified view accessed via the PWA or APK, with large touch targets (minimum 44×44px) and minimal text input.

All date inputs shall use the Gregorian calendar but display in the DD/MM/YYYY format conventional in Cameroon.

The system shall support both French (fr-CM) and English (en-CM) locale settings including number formatting and date display.

7.2 Hardware Interfaces

The system shall interface with Android device cameras for patient photograph capture.

The system shall support Bluetooth-connected weighing scales (SECA or equivalent) for automatic weight capture where available (Phase 2).

The system shall produce print-ready outputs (vaccination cards, prescriptions, referral forms) compatible with standard A4 and A5 printers.

7.3 Software Interfaces

DHIS2 Web API v2.39+: POST aggregate data values and metadata.

SMS Gateway REST API: POST outbound SMS messages; receive delivery receipts.

National NHI Registry API (Phase 2): GET patient identity verification.

Email SMTP: For system notifications and password reset emails.

7.4 Communication Interfaces

HTTPS (TLS 1.2+) for all client-server communication.

WebSockets for real-time dashboard updates and notification delivery.

REST/JSON for all API integrations.

8. Data Requirements

8.1 Data Retention

Patient clinical records shall be retained for a minimum of 25 years from the date of last activity, in line with Cameroonian health regulations.

Audit logs shall be retained for a minimum of 10 years.

Anonymised aggregate statistics shall be retained indefinitely.

8.2 Data Privacy

All personally identifiable information (PII) shall be classified as sensitive personal data and protected accordingly.

Access to individual patient records shall be limited to users directly involved in that patient's care or those with explicit supervisory or auditing roles.

Data anonymisation tools shall be provided for research data exports, removing all direct and quasi-identifiers.

The system shall maintain a complete log of all data access and export events.

8.3 Data Quality

All required fields shall be validated at point of entry with immediate feedback.

Date fields shall reject logically impossible values (e.g., future date of birth, vaccination date before date of birth).

Weight and height values shall be validated against biologically plausible ranges for age and sex, with a warning prompt for outlier values.

Duplicate patient detection shall run before any new record is saved.

8.4 Data Migration

Where facilities have existing paper or digital records, a data migration workbook (Excel template) will be provided for bulk import of historical immunisation records, ANC records, and mental health case lists. Data migration will be a supervised process conducted by trained data entry staff prior to go-live.

9. Security & Compliance

9.1 Applicable Regulations

Law No. 2010/012 on Cybersecurity and Cybercrime in Cameroon.

Law No. 96/03 of 4 January 1996 (Framework Law on Health in Cameroon).

WHO Global Code of Practice on the International Recruitment of Health Personnel.

UNICEF Child Rights and Data Protection guidelines.

HL7 FHIR R4 data standards for health interoperability.

9.2 Authentication & Authorisation

All access shall require authenticated login; no anonymous access to patient data.

RBAC shall enforce least-privilege access: users receive only the permissions required for their role.

Privileged actions (user creation, system configuration, data export) shall require MFA.

OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect shall be used for API authentication.

9.3 Data Protection

Patient data at rest: AES-256 encryption using database-level encryption.

Patient data in transit: TLS 1.2+ for all network communication; HSTS enforced.

Database backups: encrypted before storage; encryption keys stored separately from data.

Mobile offline store: encrypted local SQLite database using SQLCipher on Android.

9.4 Audit & Monitoring

All CRUD operations on patient records shall be logged with user ID, timestamp, record ID, and action type.

Audit logs shall be write-once (immutable) and stored separately from the operational database.

The system shall generate security alerts for: repeated failed login attempts, unusual after-hours access patterns, bulk data exports, and privilege escalation attempts.

A security information and event management (SIEM) integration point shall be provided for Phase 2.

9.5 Vulnerability Management

Third-party dependencies shall be scanned for known vulnerabilities using automated tooling (e.g., npm audit, OWASP Dependency-Check) in the CI/CD pipeline.

An independent penetration test shall be conducted before go-live using OWASP Top 10 as the assessment framework.

A responsible disclosure policy and contact channel shall be published.

10. Constraints & Assumptions

10.1 Constraints

Data Sovereignty: All patient data servers must be physically located within Cameroon. Cloud hosting using AWS or Azure is permissible only if Cameroonian data residency zones are used.

Official Languages: The system must support French and English as co-equal official languages; neither may be treated as secondary.

Network Infrastructure: The system must be designed for reliable operation at 2G/GPRS speeds (≥ 50 kbps) for mobile users, with graceful degradation rather than failure.

Budget: Open-source or government-licensed software must be preferred to control total cost of ownership; commercial database licences require ministerial approval.

Clinical Protocols: Clinical decision logic must strictly follow WHO mhGAP v2.0 and MINSANTE-approved EPI schedules; deviations require documented approval from MINSANTE.

Hardware: The system must not require new hardware procurement at facility level beyond a single Android device; it must run on devices as old as Android 8.0 (2017-era hardware).

10.2 Assumptions

MINSANTE will designate a technical focal point and Project Steering Committee to review and approve requirements before development begins.

A national DHIS2 instance is operational and accessible via the DHIS2 Web API v2.39+.

The SMS aggregator integration will support Unicode (UTF-8) messages for bilingual content.

Participating health facilities will have at least one staff member with basic Android smartphone literacy prior to training.

The national NHI scheme will be available by the time Phase 2 of the project begins.

MINSANTE will provide official translations of all clinical forms and instructions in both French and English.
